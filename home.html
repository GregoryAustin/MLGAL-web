Deep Learning Methods for Galaxy Detection in the Zone of Avoidance

Research into the identification of galaxies in the Zone of Avoidance.
Authors: David Jones and Gregory Austin.
Supervised by Geoff Nitschke.

Background

The Zone of Avoidance (ZoA) prohibits astronomers from easily cataloguing galaxies due to its high star density, thus an automated identificaion process is necessary. Two deep learning approaches, semi-supervised (Gregory Austin) and supervised (David Jones) were used. The semi-supervised approach used an autoencoder with Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) while for the supervised approach, a program named Galyxi Vysion was developed which uses an Evolutionary Algorithm (EA) to evolve the topologies of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). The results indicated that both learning methods were effective using their respective test sets (>90%). For more details check the research web pages.

Project Objectives

▪ Objectives for both approaches included; accurately identify galaxies in the ZoA, dealing with "noise" from other light sources in the ZoA and potentially offering an improved work flow to astronomers in helping identifying galaxies in the ZoA.

▪ The main objective of the supervised approach (David Jones) was to evaluate if a topologically evolved CNN can produce a specific CNN configuration that is able to identify galaxies in the ZoA, given its input dimensions, and if the results of using an EA are worth the processing time with respect to a human adjusted configuration.

▪ The main objective of the semi-supervised approach was to evaluate the effectiveness of using an autoencoder with ANNs (semi-supervised) to identify galaxies in the ZoA compared to only using ANNs (supervised).

Results and Conclusions

Supervised approach

▪ The best results achieved 91.75% accuracy.

▪ The best CNN configuration could not be improved upon by a human configuration.

▪ The approach was shown to be a possible tool for astronomers and could evolve a CNN that could identify potential galaxies in unseen data.

For more details on the supervised approach click here.

Semi-supervised approach

▪ The best results achieved 96% accuracy.

▪ It is theorised that the Encoder from the autoencoder learnt some useful representations because it was trained on an unlabeled dataset.

▪ Using these representations the semi-supervised learning method could utilize the data better than the supervised methods without the Encoder.
