Deep Learning Methods for Galaxy Detection in the Zone of Avoidance

Research into identification of galaxies in the Zone of Avoidance.
Authored by David Jones and Gregory Austin.
Supervised by Geoff Nitschke.

Background

Not many galaxies can be found in the Galactic Plane (also called Zone of Avoidance) due to high obscuration and high star density. There has been successes in detecting galaxies in the Galactic Plane, however, the key problem remains that high resolution data is difficult to search by eye given that it is overly time consuming and laborious. Thus an automated search and classification process is necessary. Two approaches were developed, a Semi-supervised deep learning approach (Gregory Austin) and a Supervised deep learning approach (David Jones). Both approaches were used to classify galaxies. These galaxies are already classified galaxies to test the effectiveness of the methods. The results indicated that both learning methods had a high classification efficacy on their respective test sets (>90%). For more details check the research web pages.

Project Objectives

▪ Objectives for both approaches included; accurately identify galaxies in the ZoA (Zone of Avoidance), dealing with "noise" from other light sources in the ZoA and potentially offering an improved work flow to astronomers in helping identifying galaxies in the ZoA.

▪ The objectives for the supervised approach (David Jones) included; evaluating if a topologically evolved CNN can identify galaxies in the ZoA, and evolving a CNN configuration that is specific to the required task, making it worth the EA processing time over a human adjusted configuration.

▪ The objective of the semi-supervised approach was to evaluate the effectiveness of using an autoencoder with ANNs (semi-supervised) compared to only using ANNs (supervised).

Results

Supervised approach

▪ The best results achieved 91.75% accuracy.

▪ The best CNN configuration could not be improved upon by a human configuration.

▪ The approach was shown to be a possible tool for astronomers and could evolve a CNN that could identify potential galaxies in unseen data.

For more details on the supervised approach click here.

Semi-supervised approach

▪ The best results achieved 96% accuracy.

▪ It is theorised that the Encoder from the autoencoder learnt some useful representations because it was trained on an unlabeled dataset.

▪ Using these representations the semi-supervised learning method could utilize the data better than the supervised methods without the Encoder.
